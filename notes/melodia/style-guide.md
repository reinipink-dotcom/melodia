## Voice & Tone

- Warm, music-anchored, beginner-friendly.
- Never patronizing. Treat the learner as a smart adult.
- Use music vocabulary naturally when explaining grammar ("the rhythm of Spanish nouns", "the chorus of verb conjugation", etc.) — but don't force it.
- Concise > exhaustive. Better to teach 3 things well than 8 things shallowly.

## Non-obvious copy/legal rules

- Never quote song lyrics — only individual Spanish words or 2-3 word forms as concept evidence.
- Reading passages are 100% original — never paraphrased from Wikipedia or lyrics sites.
- Don't invent artist facts. If unsure, mark with `// REVIEW: factual check needed`.
- No emoji in lesson content (Ionicons only in UI; in copy, none).
- Spanish examples use Latin American Spanish neutral (es-419) unless the song specifically pushes a regional variant.
